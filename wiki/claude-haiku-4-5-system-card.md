# Claude Haiku 4.5 System Card

## Summary

The October 2025 system card for [[Claude Haiku 4.5]], [[Anthropic]]'s small / fast tier model positioned as a hybrid-reasoning LLM with strong coding and computer-use performance at Haiku-tier cost. The card emphasizes safety evaluations over capability benchmarks: safeguards, agentic safety profile, broad [[AI alignment]], [[AI model welfare]], [[Reward hacking]] tendencies, and CBRN/[[CBRN misuse]] potential. Headline: large safety improvements over Claude Haiku 3.5, with the model deployed under the [[AI Safety Level 2]] standard per the [[Responsible Scaling Policy]]. This is the first card in this wiki from Anthropic's small-model tier and establishes the baseline against which later small-tier releases will be measured.

## Key Claims

- **Tier & positioning**: small / fast class; hybrid reasoning; optimized for coding tasks and computer use.
- **Training features**: [[Extended thinking mode]] is treated as a first-class capability (§1.1.2) rather than an afterthought — same architectural lever as larger models.
- **Release decision (§1.2)**: deployed under [[AI Safety Level 2]] per the [[Responsible Scaling Policy]]. ASL-2 means standard safeguards, pre-release risk evaluation, and deployment-time mitigations — no higher-tier containment needed.
- **Safety emphasis**: the card explicitly frames itself as safety-focused. Coverage includes safeguards, agentic-role safety, broad alignment, welfare, [[Reward hacking]], and weapons-misuse potential.
- **Reward hacking**: called out as an explicit evaluation axis — "finding shortcuts to complete tests" — foreshadowing the BrowseComp / HLE cheating-detection work that becomes prominent in the [[Claude Opus 4.6 System Card]] and [[Claude Sonnet 4.6 System Card]].
- **Comparative framing**: safety profile "compares favorably with other extant Anthropic models" — i.e. not just better than the prior Haiku, but competitive with larger contemporaries on safety metrics.
- **Structure**: follows the standard [[Anthropic]] card skeleton (model characteristics → release decision → safeguards → alignment → welfare → capability appendices) that later 4.6-series cards extend.
