# Dario Amodei

## Summary

**Dario Amodei** (born 1983) is an American AI researcher and entrepreneur; co-founder and CEO of [[Anthropic]] since 2021, which he started with his sister [[Daniela Amodei]] and other ex-[[OpenAI]] senior staff. His academic background is physics and biophysics (Stanford BS, Princeton PhD, Stanford School of Medicine postdoc), with a research history spanning Baidu, Google, and [[OpenAI]] (where he was VP of Research). In 2025 he was listed among *Time* magazine's 100 most influential people and named an "Architect of AI." He is known both for technical leadership at [[Anthropic]] and for prolific public essays — [[Machines of Loving Grace]] and [[Dario Amodei — The Adolescence of Technology]] being the two signature pieces — and for advocating an [[Entente strategy]] in which democracies use advanced AI for a decisive geopolitical advantage while sharing benefits with allies.

## Key Claims

- Born 1983 in San Francisco; attended Lowell High School; member of the 2000 USA Physics Olympiad team.
- BS Physics (Stanford, after transferring from Caltech); PhD in biophysics (Princeton, electrophysiology of neural circuits); postdoc at Stanford University School of Medicine.
- Industry trajectory: Baidu (Nov 2014 – Oct 2015) → Google → [[OpenAI]] (joined 2016, rose to VP of Research) → co-founded [[Anthropic]] in 2021 with [[Daniela Amodei]] and other OpenAI alumni, departing over directional differences.
- In November 2023, [[OpenAI]]'s board reportedly approached Amodei about replacing [[Sam Altman]] and/or merging the two companies; he declined.
- As CEO of [[Anthropic]], writes publicly on both AI benefits and risks; signature essays include [[Machines of Loving Grace]] (upside vision, [[Compressed 21st century]]) and [[Dario Amodei — The Adolescence of Technology]] (risk map).
- Geopolitical view: advocates an [[Entente strategy]] — a coalition of democracies applying advanced AI (including militarily) to secure a decisive advantage over authoritarian adversaries while sharing benefits with cooperating nations.
- Has had public disputes with elements of the U.S. Department of Defense over AI policy.
