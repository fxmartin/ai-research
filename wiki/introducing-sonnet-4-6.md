# Introducing Sonnet 4.6

## Summary

[[Anthropic]]'s product-announcement post for [[Claude Sonnet 4.6]], positioned as a "full upgrade" of the Sonnet tier across coding, computer use, long-context reasoning, agent planning, knowledge work, and design. The headline capability claim is that Opus-class performance on real-world economically valuable tasks is now available at Sonnet pricing ($3/$15 per million tokens, unchanged from Sonnet 4.5), with a 1M-token context window in beta and default-model status on free and pro claude.ai and Claude Cowork. The post frames Sonnet 4.6 as Anthropic's most capable Sonnet yet, explicitly preferred by early-access developers over both Sonnet 4.5 and the older Claude Opus 4.5 for everyday work. Safety evaluations are stated to be "as safe as, or safer than" other recent Claude models; the [[Anthropic]] safety team characterizes the model's character as "broadly warm, honest, prosocial, and at times funny" with "no signs of major concerns around high-stakes forms of misalignment."

## Key Claims

- **Tier & pricing**: Sonnet 4.6, $3/$15 per million input/output tokens, same as 4.5; default on free/pro claude.ai and [[Claude Code|Claude Cowork]]; 1M-token context window in beta.
- **Capability upgrade**: full upgrade across coding, computer use, long-context reasoning, agent planning, knowledge work, design. Approaches Opus-class intelligence on a range of tasks, including real-world office benchmarks (GDPval-AA).
- **Developer preference**: early-access developers prefer Sonnet 4.6 over both its predecessor Sonnet 4.5 and over Claude Opus 4.5 (the November 2025 Opus).
- **Computer use**: major jump on [[OSWorld-Verified]] — sixteen months of Sonnet-tier gains on this benchmark, with early users reporting near-human performance on tasks like navigating complex spreadsheets and multi-step web forms across tabs. Still short of most skilled humans in absolute terms.
- **Prompt injection robustness**: major improvement over Sonnet 4.5 at resisting [[Prompt injection]]; performs similarly to [[Opus 4.6]] on safety evaluations for this attack class.
- **Safety evaluations**: covered in the Sonnet 4.6 system card (separate document from this product post). Topline: "as safe as, or safer than, our other recent Claude models."
- **Character assessment**: safety researchers describe the model as warm, honest, prosocial, and funny at times — no signs of major high-stakes misalignment.
- **Product framing**: Anthropic is explicitly making the case that "performance that would have previously required reaching for an Opus-class model" is now a Sonnet-tier proposition — a pricing/performance realignment, not just an incremental model bump.
