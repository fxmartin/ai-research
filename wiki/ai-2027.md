# AI 2027

## Summary

**AI 2027** is a detailed, month-by-month scenario forecast published April 3, 2025 by [[Daniel Kokotajlo]], [[Eli Lifland]], [[Thomas Larsen]], Romeo Dean, and [[Scott Alexander]] (writing assist), projecting how [[Superintelligence]] might emerge and be governed between mid-2025 and the end of the decade. The scenario splits at a decision point into two endings — a **"slowdown"** and a **"race"** branch — starting from the same premises but diverging on how the U.S. government, frontier labs, and China respond once AI systems can conduct AI research autonomously. It is informed by ~25 tabletop exercises, feedback from 100+ experts in AI governance and technical work, trend extrapolation, and one author's prior forecasting track record (Kokotajlo's 2021 "What 2026 Looks Like" correctly anticipated chain-of-thought, inference scaling, chip export controls, and $100M training runs). The authors frame the work as predictive rather than prescriptive, and have run a bounty program soliciting alternative scenarios.

## Key Claims

- **Mid-2025 — Stumbling agents.** First wave of consumer "computer-using agents" marketed as personal assistants (order food, manage spreadsheets) struggle to get traction. Specialized coding and research agents, meanwhile, begin materially transforming professional work — taking Slack/Teams instructions, making substantial code changes, saving hours-to-days.
- **Coding agents as the leading edge.** The 2024→2025 transition is framed as AIs shifting from "follow instructions" (bullets→emails, prompts→code) to "function as employees," with autonomous coding agents as the canonical example.
- **Two endings, one fork.** The scenario is written iteratively period-by-period until divergence; the "race" ending was written first, then the authors deliberately wrote a "slowdown" alternative branch from the same premises to depict a more hopeful outcome.
- **2027 is the modal year, not the median.** A November 22, 2025 addendum clarifies that the authors do not claim to know when AGI will arrive: 2027 was their most-likely year at publication, but their median forecasts were somewhat later. Updated forecasts are maintained at aifuturesmodel.com.
- **The scenario is a focal point for debate.** The authors explicitly invite counter-scenarios and have offered thousands in prize money, treating public disagreement as a tool for calibrating forecasts over time.
- **Endorsement from Yoshua Bengio**, who recommends the scenario as a concrete vehicle for surfacing questions about emerging risks that aggregate abstract discourse misses.

- **Genre / related work**: [[Dario Amodei — Machines of Loving Grace]] (also optimistic-leaning long-form frontier-AI essay); [[Something Big Is Happening]] (shorter pulse-check piece). AI 2027 is more concrete and quantitative, structured as a timeline rather than an essay.
