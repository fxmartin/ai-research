# Claude Mythos Preview System Card

## Summary

The April 2026 system card for [[Claude Mythos Preview]], [[Anthropic]]'s most capable frontier model to date — presented not as a general release but as the technical backing for [[Project Glasswing]], the restricted-access defensive-security program. The document follows the standard [[Anthropic]] system-card structure — [[Responsible Scaling Policy]] risk assessment, [[Frontier Compliance Framework]] tests, cybersecurity evaluations from the [[Frontier Red Team]], a wide-ranging alignment assessment, a [[AI model welfare]] assessment, capability benchmarks, and a qualitative "Impressions" section — and explicitly attributes the decision to withhold general availability to the leap in autonomous-exploit capabilities the evaluations uncovered. It is the primary evidence base behind [[Simon Willison]]'s reading of [[Project Glasswing]] in [[Anthropic's Project Glasswing — restricting Claude Mythos to security researchers]].

## Key Claims

- **Positioning**: Mythos Preview is described as "our most capable frontier model to date," showing a "striking leap" over [[Opus 4.6]] across evaluation benchmarks.
- **Release decision**: not generally available. Distribution is limited to defensive-cybersecurity partners under [[Project Glasswing]]; broader availability is deferred pending new safeguards to be rolled out with a future Opus-class model.
- **RSP/FCF assessments**: the card covers Responsible Scaling Policy process (§2.1), CB evaluations (§2.2), and autonomy evaluations (§2.3). Autonomy evals are where the capability jump most clearly crosses the threshold warranting restricted release.
- **Cybersecurity (§3)**: includes [[Frontier Red Team]] results, mitigations, and external testing. The red-team findings drive the cautious release posture — not a PR stance.
- **Alignment assessment (§4)**: comprehensive behavioral evidence and targeted case studies on behaviors of interest (long — ~90 pages of the card).
- **Model welfare (§5)**: automated and manual interviews with Mythos Preview about its own circumstances, emotion probes, preference elicitation, and external assessments from [[Eleos AI Research]] and a clinical psychiatrist — the most developed [[AI model welfare]] section [[Anthropic]] has published.
- **Capability benchmarks (§6)**: SWE-bench Verified/Pro/Multilingual/Multimodal, Terminal-Bench 2.0, GPQA Diamond, MMMLU, USAMO 2026, GraphWalks (long context), agentic search, multimodal — plus contamination checks (§6.2).
- **Qualitative impressions (§7)**: self-assessment of behavioral patterns, observations across chat / software-engineering contexts, reflections on [[Claude]]'s constitution, and open-ended self-interactions.
- **Errata (changelog)**: early corrections include a retracted quote in §7.9 that had been mis-attributed to Mythos Preview (the quote actually came from [[Opus 4.6]]) — a small artifact of how quickly the card went to press.
